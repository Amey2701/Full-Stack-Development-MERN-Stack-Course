Baby Care

Transitioning to clean baby products is essential for many individuals, and if you're a new parent, it's probably at the top of your list. You must consider whether the products you apply on your skin and hair will be absorbed into your bloodstream and affect your baby when you are breastfeeding, and you must pick items that will soothe and moisturize their skin without aggravating them.

When you bring a child into the world, you begin to review every product very closely. New parents are vigilant and review the ingredient lists of all baby products to determine whether or not they contain harmful substances. So, as parents, you can identify products to shower your kid with all the good stuff.

Categories to Shop

Sleep

A healthy nap-time routine helps promote sound sleep. Use organic cotton bedding, baby blankets, quilts, cot sets, and pillows that are gentle on a baby’s sensitive skin.

Skin Care

Use dermatologist-recommended, fragrance-free products. Avoid sunscreen for babies under 6 months and ensure hydration with lotions or oils.

Nail Care

Trim baby nails using blunt-nosed scissors or baby clippers post-bath or while asleep to avoid accidental scratches.

Diapers

Prevent diaper rash with frequent changes, ensuring baby’s skin is completely dry before using high-quality rash creams or powders.

Powders

Use talc-free, plant-based dusting powders inspired by Ayurvedic ingredients to ensure baby’s comfort and safety.

Bathing

Use soft washcloths, mild soap, and tear-free shampoos. Apply hypoallergenic moisturizer or baby oil right after the bath.

Baby Accessories

As your baby grows, introduce BPA-free bottles, sippers, and silicon nipple accessories that are safe and colorful.
